About Live Free Initiative

Transforming Communities Through Youth Empowerment

Our Story

Adjumani District in West Nile Region, Uganda, has experienced rapid growth with a population exceeding 250,000 people, including over 250,000 refugees. However, this growth has been accompanied by rising social challenges including substance abuse and other social vices that accelerated during the COVID-19 pandemic.

During the 2020-2021 lockdown, schools and businesses closed, creating conditions where many young people were exposed to substance abuse, risky behaviors, and economic hardship. School dropouts increased due to financial constraints and issues like teenage pregnancy.

In response to these challenges, a group of visionary young Christian men and women came together to form Live Free Initiative—to address social issues and inspire other young people to become agents of change in their communities. The organization is gradually expanding into neighboring districts of Moyo, Yumbe, and Obongi.

Our Vision

To inspire and empower young people as catalysts for holistic and sustainable community transformation through the timeless truth of the gospel and evidence-based practices for healthy living.

Our Mission

To provide young people and families with vital knowledge and skills that drive personal and community transformation in drug-free spaces where talent and creativity flourish through fun-filled activities and stakeholder engagement.

Our Goal

To have holistically transformed communities that advantage and benefit all persons by 2050.
