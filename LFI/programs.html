Featured Program

Live Free Club

A school-based platform where young people are educated and empowered to stand up against social evils, especially substance abuse.

What is the Live Free Club?

The Live Free Club is established in secondary schools and higher institutions of learning. It serves as a key player in school orientation programs to acquaint new students with healthy, drug-free living. Club members receive training to become health leaders, peer educators, and peer counselors in their schools, families, and communities.

Key Activities

  • Creating awareness about substance abuse and addiction among learners
  • Training students as peer educators and peer counselors
  • Active learning through essays, poetry, plays, music, discussions, and drama performances
  • Edutainment sessions with educational films, documentaries, and analysis
  • Recognition and awards for outstanding members
  • Inter-school club competitions with recognition for best performers
  • Article contributions to the Live Free Magazine

Digital Content Creation

Fun, informative, and inspiring content designed to drive awareness on social media platforms where young people gather online.

The Strategy

Our digital content initiative recognizes that young people spend significant time online. Rather than avoiding these platforms, we create positive, engaging content that:

  • Counteracts negative online influences with positive messaging
  • Educates audiences about substance abuse prevention and wellness
  • Inspires content creators to produce positive content
  • Encourages productive use of social media platforms
  • Creates conversations around community transformation
